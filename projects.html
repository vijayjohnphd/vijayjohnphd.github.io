Research Projects

Guardian Robot Research

RIKEN · 2021 – 2025

Part of a six-team effort at RIKEN developing a social robot with human-like characteristics. Contributed multimodal perception modules for emotion recognition, person classification under missing-modality conditions, and audio-visual human-robot interaction.

Humanoid RoboticsMultimodal LearningEmotion Recognition

Missing Modality Problem in Multimodal Learning

RIKEN · 2021 – 2025

Proposed KModNet and a multimodal cascaded framework with metric learning to perform person classification with any arbitrary subset of modalities. Developed latent-space imputation to synthesise missing-modality features, and a multi-head attention transformer for audio-visual recognition when visual data is absent.

KModNetMetric LearningTransformersLatent Space

Multimodal Weak Supervised Learning

RIKEN · 2021 – 2025

Addressed training video-based action recognition with sequence-level (weak) labels instead of expensive frame-level annotations. Introduced a novel latent loss function that produces view-specific embeddings boosting downstream action recognition and detection.

Weak SupervisionAction RecognitionLatent Embeddings

Multimodal Learning for Intelligent Mobility

Toyota Technological Institute · Denso · Aisin · Toyota R&D · 2014 – 2021

Principal contributor across six collaborative projects. Developed RVNet and SO-Net (radar–camera fusion), ChiNet and PsiNet (stereo depth–appearance fusion for lane estimation under occlusion and shadows), adversarial frameworks for pedestrian intention prediction, and automatic LIDAR–stereo calibration without targets.

RVNetChiNet / PsiNetLIDARRADARSemantic Segmentation

Gesture Recognition & Vehicle Localisation

Toyota Technological Institute · 2016 – 2019

Deep learning + sparse modelling framework for touchless automotive gesture recognition. Particle swarm optimisation + Kalman filtering for vehicle localisation using stereo-vision 3D point clouds.

Gesture RecognitionPSO3D Point CloudLocalisation

Automatic Calibration of Non-Overlapping FOV Cameras

University of Amsterdam · Philips Research · Eagle Vision · 2011 – 2013

Probabilistic graphical model for person re-identification and extrinsic calibration across cameras with non-overlapping fields of view. Refined with a Bayesian (Metropolis) framework to correct accumulated errors.

Re-identificationBayesian InferenceMulti-camera

Human Motion Analysis Using Multiple Cameras

University of Dundee · PhD Research · 2007 – 2011

Markerless multi-view human motion tracking using particle swarm optimisation and charting-based subspace learning. Featured in Science Scotland, Royal Society of Edinburgh.
